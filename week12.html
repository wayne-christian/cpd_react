Assessment Overview Refresher

For this assessment, you will need to design and develop a mobile app and report on the iterative process of designing and developing it.

The app must revolve around a “mobile moment”.

For something to qualify as a mobile moment, it should not be possible / desirable to do the same activity with a laptop / desktop computer. Appropriate mobile moments could be (but are not limited to): commuting, waking up, being in a new city, hiking, sitting in a lecture, … This requirement has two main components: design and development.

The app should showcase a thorough understanding of mobile design considerations (e.g. user interaction, user experience) and follow an iterative development approach. Furthermore, it is important that the app goes beyond simplistic HTML pages but instead makes use of more advanced features that are a meaningful response to the mobile moment.

Supplemental Material

In support of your written report, you also need to submit your project files and a video. (NO Binaries - publish) This must be in the form of a zip archive.

Remember to link your report to the "5 planes of UX" set out in Khaleds Lecture

1. Project files: Include all files necessary to build your app. This includes all code and media files.

2. Publish to expo: You must publish your app to expo repository (covered later) and include that link in your report

3. Video: Your video needs to show your app running on at least one device (Emulator is allowed). Your video should be a maximum of 2 minutes long and showcase the core functionality of the app. You are free to use voice over or subtitles for explanations. Your video file must not be larger than 100MB and in MP4.
